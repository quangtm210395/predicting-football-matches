# World Cup 2026 — context factors that move probabilities

Verify specifics against live sources before each match — schedules, venues, and
qualification scenarios change. This file is the checklist of *what to look for*,
not a substitute for current data.

## Format (different from past World Cups)

- **48 teams, 12 groups of 4.** Top 2 of each group **plus the 8 best third-placed
  teams** advance to a **Round of 32**, then standard knockout to the Final
  (MetLife Stadium, New Jersey, ~19 July 2026).
- **Implication for the 3rd group game:** because 8 of 12 third-placed teams
  progress, the math of "what result do we need" is complex. By matchday 3 a team
  may be already-through, already-out, or needing an exact result. This creates
  **dead rubbers, rotated XIs, and must-win desperation** — all of which distort
  form-based models. Always check the live group table and qualification
  permutations before matchday 3 games.
- **26-man squads** → more rotation, especially for favourites managing load.

## Hosts & venues — heat, altitude, roof

Matches are spread across **USA (11 cities), Mexico (3), Canada (2)** in June–July
(northern-hemisphere summer). Three physical factors materially affect goals and
fatigue:

| Factor | Where it matters | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Altitude** | Mexico City (~2,240 m), Guadalajara (~1,560 m) | Tires sea-level teams, favours lower tempo; Mexico is acclimatised |
| **Heat / humidity** | Daytime kickoffs in Monterrey, Miami, Houston, Dallas, Kansas City, Guadalajara, Atlanta | Slows pace, lowers pressing intensity → can suppress goals; check kickoff local time |
| **Roof / A/C stadiums** | AT&T (Dallas), NRG (Houston), Mercedes-Benz (Atlanta), SoFi (LA), BC Place (Vancouver) | Climate-controlled — neutralises heat; don't apply a heat discount here |

- **Travel & time zones:** the three countries span multiple time zones (ET → PT,
  plus Mexico). A team criss-crossing the map between group games carries more
  fatigue and jet-lag than one with a tight regional cluster — check each side's
  itinerary and rest days.

## Venue advantage

- All sites are **neutral**, except the **host nations** (USA, Mexico, Canada) get
  a real crowd advantage when playing at home. Treat host-nation home games like a
  modest home-field edge; treat everything else as neutral.

## What to pull for each match

1. Current group table + exact qualification scenario for both teams (motivation).
2. Kickoff **local time** and venue → heat/altitude/roof adjustment.
3. Each team's **rest days** since last match and **travel** between venues.
4. Whether a favourite is likely to **rotate** (already qualified, load management).
